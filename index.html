AccuChats
AI reliability platform for enterprise LLM deployments

Enterprises are deploying LLM-powered assistants on their own data — internal docs, product knowledge bases, support systems. The problem they all hit: the AI gives confident, wrong answers. AccuChats is the evaluation and reliability layer that fixes this before it reaches users.

Built and measured so far
  • Evaluated 50+ prompt templates across 4 embedding models
  • Improved grounded answer accuracy from 72% → 96% using hybrid retrieval + reranking
  • Built eval framework across 400+ benchmark queries — measuring hallucination risk, retrieval precision, and response consistency
  • Shipped entire platform in 30 days, ~85% AI-assisted via Cursor.ai
accuchats.com ↗
The problem I am solving

Most LLM deployment failures are not model failures. They are retrieval failures — the AI never received the right context to answer correctly in the first place.

The 72% → 96% accuracy jump in AccuChats came entirely from fixing the retrieval layer: tuning retrieval count, adding metadata filters, and expanding queries before the LLM ran. The model never changed.

Enterprises spending months on prompt engineering are solving the wrong problem. AccuChats surfaces the right one.

AI-Personalised Newsletter Platform
10M+ daily users · Angel One

Built an AI-driven newsletter system that delivered personalised daily market content to 10M+ users. The personalisation engine selected content topics, timing, and format based on each user's trading behaviour and feature usage — not a single template for all.

The decision that mattered

Early versions sent the same content to all users with mild segmentation. Engagement was flat. I rebuilt the content selection layer to predict which market topics each user actually cared about based on their holdings and past interactions. Open rates and downstream app engagement both moved — proving that personalisation only works when the signal is right, not just when the timing is optimised.

2024
Perfect Ping — ML Push Targeting
XGBoost targeting system · Angel One

Built an ML targeting system that decided who to send push notifications to, what to send, and when — replacing manual segmentation with a model that predicted each user's next most likely action.

The decision that mattered

The platform had severe notification fatigue. Instead of A/B testing send times, I built an XGBoost model predicting which feature a user was most likely to activate next and only notified when there was something genuinely relevant for that user. Push CTR nearly tripled because we stopped treating 1.5M users as one cohort.

0.7% → 2% push CTR
+5% DAU traffic

Things I actually believe

  • The retrieval layer matters more than the model.
    GPT-4 with bad retrieval underperforms GPT-3.5 with excellent grounding. Most AI failures in production are retrieval failures nobody diagnosed correctly.
  • Chat is not the end state for AI products.
    We are in the everything-is-a-chat-interface phase. Structured decision systems will replace most of what we currently call AI products within 5 years.
  • AI reliability will become a compliance requirement.
    The moment AI touches a regulated workflow, hallucination probability becomes a legal question. Most enterprises are not ready for this conversation.
  • Most ML personalisation optimises for the wrong signal.
    Models trained on clicks optimise for clicks. Without measuring what happened after the click, you build a system that gets better at the wrong thing.
